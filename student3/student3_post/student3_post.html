Nine Arch Bridge is a famous landscape between Ella and Demodara in Sri Lanka.Set against a backdrop of thick jungle and tea plantations, Ella’s Nine Arch Bridge is actually one among the foremost beautiful sights that Sri Lanka has got to offer. When the mist hangs low over the encompassing mountains, and therefore the iconic blue Sri Lankan train passes sleepily along the tracks, the place takes on an almost mystical feel and it’s easy to imagine you're on the set of a movie or have walked from the pages of a completely unique.
The Nine Arch Bridge has become one among Ella’s most visited attractions, and it’s easy to ascertain why, but it’s still amazingly hard to seek out for such a well-known attraction and that we very nearly didn’t make it at all! Luckily, albeit we went completely the incorrect way, we still managed to scramble through the jungle and made it eventually. Not only is Nine Arch Bridge incredibly beautiful, but it's also one among the simplest samples of colonial-era construction and features a design that architects marvel at.
The bridge was built at Gotuwala between the Ella and Demodara stations during the British Colonial period. The story goes that shortly after the Nine Arch Bridge had been commissioned by the British, WW1 broke out.
They couldn’t possibly justify using steel to create the bridge, because it was needed much more for the war efforts, so a team of Sri Lankan engineers and builders completed the work in 1921 using blocks of stone and cement. to the present day, the 91m long, 24m high bridge has stood entirely without steel support! We love this tiny little bit of history and think it makes The Nine Arch Bridge even more special.
We ponder whether the engineers and builders who bought this bridge to life could possibly have guessed that today it might be a favorite spot for travelers and locals alike, who come to wave at the passengers because the blue Sri Lankan train snakes it’s way along the railway to cheers and smile.
Locally it's referred to as ‘Ahas Namaye Palama’ meaning nine skies bridge in Sinhala because once you stand below the bridge you'll see the sky through the nine arches. There are many various ways of going to Nine Arch Bridge in Ella. you'll get to choose the choice that suits your needs best, counting on time, mobility and money.
Tuk-tuk to Nine Arch Bridge | you'll grab a tuk-tuk from the most street in Ella town. it'll take you most of the thanks to the bridge then you'll walk the last bit on foot.
This is definitely the quickest and easiest route, though not the simplest for scenery and taking within the atmosphere. If, however, it starts pouring down with rain and you are expecting it'll be a fast shower then this could be the right time to urge there quick. Tuk-tuks cost about LKR 200-300 for this journey.
Walking to Nine Arch Bridge | you'll walk to Nine Arch Bridge. There are literally many various routes to the bridge. we've detailed the most and easiest route here, but if you fancy a touching trek through the jungle, and you've got time to spare just in case you finish up down a dead-end, then why not try mapping out a route of your own on google?
