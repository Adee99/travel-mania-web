Temple of the Tooth
By Adeepa

-- World Heritage --


Sri Dalada Maligawa or the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic is a Buddhist temple within the town of Kandy, Sri Lanka. it's set within the royal palace complex of the previous Kingdom of Kandy, that homes the relic of the tooth of religious mystic. Since history, the relic has vie an important role in native politics as a result of it's believed that whoever holds the relic holds the governance of the country.
Kandy was the last capital of the Sri Lankan kings and is a World Heritage area principally because of the temple. Bhikkhus of the two chapters of Malwatte and Asgiriya conduct daily worship within the inner chamber of the temple. Rituals are performed 3 times daily: at dawn, at twelve noon and within the evenings.
On Wednesdays, there's a symbolic bathing of the relic with a flavorer preparation made of scented water and sweet flowers referred to as Nanumura Mangallaya. This water is believed to contain healing powers and is distributed among those present. The golden cover made over the most shrine of the temple is viewed from distant whereas the recent royal court of King Vimaladarmasuriya I remains AN imposing a testament to the imperial rule of the ancient city.
